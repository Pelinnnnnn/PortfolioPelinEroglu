BETTER TRUST THE COMMUNITY

the future of an abondent constructionsite

Pelin Eroğlu Dario Fiege Paula Rönnau Prof. Dr. Gabu Heindl WM Iva Marčetić WM Florine Schüschke Universität Kassel FG Architecture Cities Economies PRO Ruins of Speculation

ANALYSIS

Signa is bankrupt - What kind of Elbtower does Hamburg need?

There is currently strong pressure on the neighbouring districts to the east, both on cultural initiatives and on the housing market, due to the development starting in the Hafencity area. There are times of upheaval ahead, the effects of which are already being felt.

Rents per square metre in Hamburg are very high on average, especially in the area of particularly small and large flats. Neighbourhoods such as Rothenburgsort and Veddel still offer affordable living space. However, with the forthcoming changes due to urban redevelopment and urban renewal projects, the signs are pointing to rising rents.

Initiatives in Hamburg

After HafenCity, the Rotheburgsort, Hammerbrook and Veddel areas are now on the „urban upgrading“ programme. Various master plans and construction plans are threatening the existence of places of culture and togetherness and thus the livelihoods of many artists and cultural workers, founders and committed people. The wave of displacement and destruction of these important places only stops at the very few. As a result, the majority of the initiatives involved in the „AG Ost“ association are currently under acute threat of eviction or have already been evicted. At least 520 people are involved in these initiatives and shared locations.
