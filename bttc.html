BETTER TRUST THE COMMUNITY

the future of an aboundend construction site

In collaboration with Dario Fiege and Paula Rönnau
Supervised by Dr Prof Gabu Heindl, Iva Marčetić (M.A.) and Florine Schüschke (M.A.)

This project reimagines the halted Elbtower development in Hamburg as a community-led space in response to growing displacement in the surrounding districts of Rothenburgsort, Hammerbrook, and Veddel. These areas, rich in cultural and social initiatives, are under increasing threat from urban redevelopment plans and rising rents. Many grassroots organizations—members of the AG Ost network—face eviction, despite their vital contributions to the city's cultural and social fabric.
In contrast to the profit-driven investor logic that shaped the original Elbtower project, our design proposes municipal repurchase of the land and its management through a Community Land Trust (CLT). This model ensures long-term affordability and democratic control. A cooperative housing structure integrates cultural, residential, and working spaces, giving stability to a diverse mix of users.
The design approach preserves the unfinished state of the Elbtower—such as the exposed building core and partial façade—making the failure of speculative real estate development visible. The architectural expression embraces these historical breaks, treating them as opportunities for reinterpretation rather than erasure.
Public life is reactivated at ground level through an open, transparent base with exhibition spaces, a club, and access to the Elbe. The first upper levels feature communal areas: theaters, auditoriums, a cinema, and a cooperative cafeteria—serving as the heart of the project. New circulation paths, niches, and layered transitions create lively interactions and a strong spatial identity.
This speculative proposal envisions the Elbtower not as a monument to failed investment, but as a resilient urban landmark for collective use, solidarity, and cultural continuity.
