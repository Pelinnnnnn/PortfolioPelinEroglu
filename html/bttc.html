Analysis

Signa is bankrupt - What kind of Elbtower does Hamburg need?

There is currently strong pressure on the neighbouring districts to the east, both on cultural initiatives and on the housing market, due to the development starting in the Hafencity area. There are times of upheaval ahead, the effects of which are already being felt.

Rents per square metre in Hamburg are very high on average, especially in the area of particularly small and large flats. Neighbourhoods such as Rothenburgsort and Veddel still offer affordable living space. However, with the forthcoming changes due to urban redevelopment and urban renewal projects, the signs are pointing to rising rents.
